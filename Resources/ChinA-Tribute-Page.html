Chinua Achebe

(16/11/1930 - 21/3/2013)

"One of the truest tests of integrity is its blunt refusal to be compromised."

A timeline of Chinua Achebe's life.

  • 1930 - 1947: Albert Chinualumogu Achebe is born in Ogidi region of Nigeria. He attended St Philips' Central School in Ogidi for primary education before enrolling at Nekede Central School where he passed entrance exams for two colleges.
  • 1948 - 1953: Chinua Achebe is accepted to University College(now the University of Ibadan) to study medicine but later changes to English, history and theology after realizing that existing English literature misrepresented Nigerian characters.After graduation, he takes a teaching position at the Merchants of Light school in Oba.
  • 1953 - 1956: He worked in Oba for four months before he was offered a position as a scriptwriter by the Nigerian Broadcasting Service(NBS) where he had an opportunity to attend the Staff School offered by the British Broadcasting Corporation(BBC) in London.
  • 1957 - 1960: In 1957 he starts working on his first novel, Things fall apart and on 17 june 1958, the first 2000 copies of the book are published. In 1960, he publishes No longer at ease and later that year is awarded a Rockefeller Fellowship for six months of touring and lecturing in west Africa.
  • 1961 - 1967: Upon his return to Nigeria in 1961, Achebe is promoted at NBS to Director of External Broadcasting where he was responsible for the creation of the Voice of Nigeria(VON) network. In 1964, he publishes his third book, Arrow of God, followed by A man of the people in 1966.
  • 1967 - 1975: Between 1967 and 1970, Achebe was heavily involved in the Nigeria-Biafra war so writing took a backseat. After the war in 1975, Achebe took a job at the University of Nigeria and helped start two magazines, Okike and Nsukkascope. In 1972 Achebe was offered professorship at the University of Massachusetts Amherst and moved his family to the United States.
  • 1976 - 1986: In 1976 Achebe returns to Nigeria to be a professor at the University of Nigeria. In 1986, while working on his novel he is elected president-general of the Ogidi Town Union and has a three-year term.
  • 1987 - 1999: In 1987, his novel, Anthills of the Savannah is published. In 1990 Achebe is involved in a car accident that paralyzes him from the waist down. In 1991, he takes a job as a Professor of Languages and Literature at Bard College in New York where he stays for the next 15 years.
  • 2000 - 2013: In 2007, he was awarded the Man Booker International Prize and he published a collection of essays named Education of a British-protected Child. He joined Brown University as David and Marianna Fisher University Professor of Africana Studies. In March of 2013, Achebe dies after a short illness.

Notable works.
